all about lilies 

what they are  

Lilies are flowering plants known for their large, colorful, and often fragrant blooms. They belong to the genus Lilium and grow from bulbs planted in the ground. Lilies come in many varieties and colors, including white, pink, yellow, orange, and red. These flowers are popular in gardens and floral arrangements because of their beauty and elegant shape. Lilies thrive in well-drained soil and sunny locations, and they attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. In many cultures, lilies symbolize purity, renewal, and devotion.

  

How they are made

They grow naturally from underground bulbs. A lily bulb stores nutrients that help the plant begin growing when conditions are right. Roots grow from the bulb into the soil, while a stem grows upward toward the sunlight. As the plant develops, leaves form along the stem and a flower bud appears. The bud gradually opens into a lily flower. Lilies can also reproduce by producing seeds after pollination or by growing new bulbs, which can develop into new lily plants. Proper sunlight, water, and nutrients help lilies grow healthy and produce beautiful blooms.

fun facts

  1. Some lilies can grow over 2 metres (6 feet) tall.
  2. Lilies come in many colours, including white, pink, yellow, orange, and red.
  3. Their sweet scent helps attract bees and butterflies.
  4. Lilies have been grown by people for thousands of years and are featured in many ancient artworks and stories.
